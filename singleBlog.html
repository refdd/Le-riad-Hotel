Prince Mohamed Ali Palace - History of the Manial Palace
A museum as well as the palace of Prince Mohamed Ali Manial is a magnificent exhibit of Egypt's history. It originated with Alawiyya Empire, the period during which Muhammad Ali in its place ruled Egypt. It is located in the southern Cairo neighborhood of Manial. Palaces and mansions were kept in excellent condition in recent decades, keeping their original beauty intact. In this article, we will discuss the history of the Manial Palace.
Let’s get started..
History of the Palace
Building work on the Manial Palace took place between 1899 and 1929 during the reign of Prince Mohamed Ali (1875-1955), King Farouk's uncle. Founder of the Persian Empire, he was the second child and grandchild of Khedive Ismail, as well as a brother of Al-Abbas Hilmi II. He was born in Cairo, Egypt, on November 9, 1875. With a curiosity for learning, he attended Abdeen High School before he traveled throughout Europe to graduate from Swiss high school Hyksos before he studied at Terzianum School in Austria. After his father died in 1892, he decided to study military science. He later headed back to Egypt. He was always known to be a smart guy with a passion for literature, art, science, and a great amount of knowledge. He built such a superb palace that it clearly explains how he did it.

Then & Now of the Manial Palace
Prince Mohammed Ali held numerous parties throughout the royal period to entertain top pashas, ministers, dignitaries, artists, and journalists. After his death, he asked for the Palace to be preserved as a museum. In 1952, they seized the assets belonging to Muhammad Ali Pasha, and the mansion became a museum, allowing the public to view the splendor in which they lived. The Palace in 2020, in celebration of 117 years of existence, held a special art exhibition in which they put on several of the Palace's most distinguished works of art were displayed in the main hall.
The Museum
A public art and history museum has been built in the Manial Palace that exhibits artwork. It stores a wide range of objects including pieces of art, silver, and clothing. Among the items donated by the family of Mohamed Ali Pasha were texts, drawings, gems, and candlesticks donated in 1955. The Museum is on the south of the Vatican and includes fifteen halls spread out between small courtyards. During the time when King Farouk was alive, the Hunting Museum he owned was open to the public. In 1963, the museum opened its doors, consisting of 1180 exhibits, such as preserved butterflies and animals collected by Mohamed Ali, Yusef Kamal, and Farouk, as well as the skeleton of a horse from the possession caravan on its way to the Kaaba.
Gardens of the Royal Palace
The palace's name indicates that the gardens are within a radius of approximately 34 miles. The collection contains plants and trees acquired by Prince Mohamed Ali around the globe, such as cacti and fig plants, as well as palm trees and bamboo Tourists can enjoy viewing these beautiful parks and gardens as well as the exotic tropical plants that the Prince collected. A King Who Never Was
Mohammed Ali became well known because he ruled as king three times. Shortly after becoming crown prince during the reign of his brother Khedive, Britain asked him to leave the country, so he traveled to Monterrey, where Sultan Ahmed Fuad I granted permission to return home. It was his honor to be appointed as crown prince once again. After that, Sultan had a son named Prince Farouk. The Egyptian monarch chose him as one of the three guardians of the royal throne after the death of Ahmed Fouad II, he attended the Coronation of King George VI on behalf of Egypt during that period.
